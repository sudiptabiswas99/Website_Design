§03Read your house

Six things the soil does before it gets your attention.

One of these on its own is often nothing. Two or three together usually mean the foundation has moved. None of them are urgent enough to sign a contract over the phone.

01
Cracks that climb the brickStair-step cracks stepping up through mortar joints, or cracks running out from the corners of windows and doors.
02
Doors and windows that stickA door that used to latch and now drags, or a fresh gap opening at the top corner of the frame.
03
Floors that slope or bounceA floor that reads downhill across the room, or a soft spot that gives a little when you cross it.
04
Trim pulling awayBaseboards, crown molding, or countertops separating from the wall as the framing racks out of square.
05
A wall that leans or bowsA basement or retaining wall bulging inward, or a chimney tilting away from the house.
06
Slab and driveway cracksWide cracks in a garage slab, patio, or driveway that keep lengthening season after season.

§08Where we work

We know Metroplex clay because we dig in it every day.

The soil under Dallas is mostly expansive clay. It swells when it is wet and shrinks when it dries, and that cycle is exactly what moves foundations here. We work it across the metro.

It depends on how far the home has settled, how many piers it needs, and how deep firm soil sits. A small repair runs a few thousand dollars, and a large multi-pier job runs well into five figures.
