Know before you book

Soft wash or pressure wash?

They are not the same job, and using the wrong one can do real damage. Here is the short version of how we decide on every surface.

Soft wash

Low pressure plus a cleaning solution

The solution does the work, so the pressure stays low. It lifts algae and mildew at the root, which is why a soft wash tends to stay clean longer than a blast that only knocks off the surface.

  • House siding, stucco, and painted surfaces
  • Roof shingles with black algae streaks
  • Screen enclosures and softer materials

Pressure wash

High pressure for hard surfaces

For concrete and stone we use higher pressure through a flat surface cleaner. That keeps the finish even and pulls up the ground-in gray that a wand alone leaves behind in stripes.

  • Concrete driveways and walkways
  • Pavers, patios, and pool decks
  • Brick, block, and retaining walls

The short rule we follow: hard flatwork gets pressure, everything attached to your house gets a soft wash. We pick the method per surface so nothing gets etched, gouged, or stripped.
