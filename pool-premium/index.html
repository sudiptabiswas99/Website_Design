Three ways to build

Gunite, fiberglass, or vinyl.

Each build type has a different cost, timeline, and feel. On your design call we walk through which one fits your yard, your budget, and how soon you want to swim.

01

Gunite concrete

8 to 12+ weeks

Sprayed concrete shells shaped to any design with no size or form limits. The choice for custom features, beach entries, and built-in spas.

02

Fiberglass

3 to 6 weeks

A factory-made shell set in a few weeks, with a smooth surface that resists algae. The fastest way from contract to swimming.

03

Vinyl liner

6 to 8 weeks

A custom-framed pool with a replaceable liner, soft underfoot and friendly on the budget. A practical way into pool ownership.

Questions before you build

The ones we hear on every design call.

Straight answers on cost, timing, and how a pool actually gets built.

It depends heavily on the build type, the size, and the features. As a national range, vinyl pools tend to start around the lower end, fiberglass sits in the middle, and a fully custom gunite build with spa, decking, and water features reaches the top. Pricing varies by region and scope. We give you a real range for your specific yard on the free 3D design call.

Gunite is fully custom with no shape limits, takes the longest, and costs the most. Fiberglass is the fastest to install with a smooth, low-maintenance surface, but comes in set shapes. Vinyl is the friendliest on budget and soft underfoot, with a liner that gets replaced over the years. We walk through the trade-offs for your yard so the choice is clear.

From the start of construction, fiberglass runs roughly 3 to 6 weeks, vinyl about 6 to 8 weeks, and gunite 8 to 12 weeks or more for a complex build. Permitting and inspections add time before and during. We give you a realistic schedule for your project up front.

Yes. We work with pool-loan lenders, and pre-qualifying takes minutes with a soft credit pull that does not affect your score. Many clients finance the full build so they can lock this year's pricing and pay over time. We go over the real terms on your design call.
