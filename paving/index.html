FAQ

What people ask before they pave.

The questions that come up on most estimates.

Most driveways run somewhere around 7 to 15 dollars per square foot, depending on the size, how much tear-out and base work it needs, and access for the equipment. Pricing varies by region. We measure the job and give you a written number before any work starts.

Plan on staying off it for about 72 hours, longer in hot weather. The surface keeps curing for the first 6 to 12 months, so it stays a little soft. Avoid parking in the same spot every day and keep sharp tires and jack stands off it early on.

The first seal coat usually goes on 6 to 12 months after the driveway is paved, once it has fully cured. After that, every 2 to 3 years keeps the surface protected from sun, water, and salt. We offer it as a maintenance package so you don't have to remember to call.

A driveway that's paved on a good base and kept sealed and crack-filled often lasts around 15 to 20 years. Skipping maintenance is what cuts that short, because water gets into cracks and freezes the surface apart over Ohio winters.

Asphalt goes down best in warm, dry weather, roughly 50 degrees and up, which means late spring through fall in central Ohio. We book up through the season, so it helps to call early. Sealcoating and crack work are best done before winter sets in.

Asphalt usually costs less up front, goes down fast, and is easy to repair and reseal. Concrete lasts longer with less maintenance but costs more and cracks are harder to fix cleanly. For most driveways in this climate, asphalt is the practical pick. We're happy to walk you through both.
