# Premium Plumbing Website — Research Playbook
**Subject:** Hargrove & Sons (34-yr family-owned, Sacramento) — build premium, human-designed, NOT AI/template.
**Method:** Multi-agent deep research — 6 angles, 26 sources fetched, 127 claims extracted, 25 adversarially verified (3-vote), **11 confirmed / 14 killed**. Run `wf_334aef19-007`, 109 agents.
**Rule:** Only claims that survived verification are below. Refuted "stats" are listed at the bottom — do NOT use them.

---

## 1 · Typography
- **Avoid AI-default fonts:** Inter, Poppins, Montserrat, Lato, Open Sans, Roboto. Their ubiquity makes a design "read as default rather than chosen." ✅ verified (3-0) · ⚠️ blog-level, 2026-dated, re-check before launch.
  - Sources: madegooddesigns.com/best-sans-serif-fonts, Medium/Bootcamp "Inter fatigue", ux.prithivkumar.com
- **What premium brands do:** license foundry grotesques. **Söhne** (Klim Type Foundry, Kris Sowersby, 2019) is used by **Stripe, OpenAI, Medium**. ✅ verified (3-0) · HIGH (Klim + Fonts In Use primary).
  - Sources: typewolf.com/sohne, klim.co.nz/fonts/soehne, fontsinuse.com/uses/35338 & /52248, klim.co.nz/in-use/openai-devday
- **Free equivalents (the practical move):** body = **Satoshi / General Sans / Geist** (Söhne-class) · display = **Fraunces / Instrument Serif** (heritage serif). ⚠️ practitioner mapping, not directly sourced.
- ❌ Refuted: "Hanken Grotesk is the best free Söhne clone" (0-3); "Söhne used by Mercury/Linear" (0-3). Don't claim a single best clone.
- **Decision for Hargrove:** **Fraunces (display) + Satoshi (body)** is the research's literal recommendation. (Drop Inter from earlier picks.)

## 2 · Lead & Booking Forms
- **More fields → gradual (not steep) conversion drop** — analysis of 40,000+ landing pages; be especially cautious with **textareas and select boxes** vs single-line inputs. ✅ verified (3-0) · HIGH.
  - Source: blog.hubspot.com (which-types-of-form-fields-lower-landing-page-conversions); corroborated Neil Patel, Eloqua.
- **Do NOT blindly strip fields:** an A/B test *lost* 14% by cutting fields, then *gained +19.21%* by restoring relevant ones and **testing the labels**. Expedia removed one optional "Company" field → **+$12M/yr**. ✅ verified (3-0; Expedia 2-1) · HIGH.
  - Sources: ventureharbour.com/how-form-length-impacts-conversion-rates, uxmovement.com/the-12-million-optional-form-field, cxl.com/blog/reduce-form-fields
- **Takeaway:** keep only relevant fields (name, phone, address/service, issue), remove flow-breakers, test label clarity over raw brevity.
- ❌ Refuted: "multi-step forms = 300%/743%/226% lift", "3-4 field rule", "~20% drop per field over 5". Do NOT build a long multi-step form on those.

## 3 · Site Structure & Page Architecture
- **Each core service gets its OWN dedicated page** (emergency, water heater, drain, repipe, sewer) — not one combined Services page. **#1 local-SEO ranking factor** (Whitespark, 47 experts). ✅ verified (3-0) · HIGH. Each page needs unique substantive content (thin/duplicate pages hurt).
  - Sources: cspmarketingsolutions.com/blog/hvac-plumbing-website-design, whitespark.ca/local-search-ranking-factors
- **Conversion paths:** header CTA (Book Now / Request Service), phone in header, **24/7 emergency visible in ~3s**, real-time online scheduling, chat-to-text widget, sticky Book-Now / tap-to-call. ✅ verified (3-0) · HIGH (ServiceTitan + Nopio + WebCitz + Comrade).
- **Mobile hero (first ~600px) = 4 things:** (1) headline `service + city + benefit` matching search intent, (2) tap-to-call, (3) three trust signals (Google rating, license #, **years in business**), (4) **one real photo**. ✅ verified (3-0) · components backed by stronger CRO sources (message-match, phone-leads-convert-3-12x).
  - Sources: pipelineon.com/blog/contractor-landing-page-tips, Unbounce (dedicated LPs ~65% higher conversion)
- ❌ Refuted: sticky-bar "25-40% lift" number; ServiceTitan's specific "section order" list.

## 4 · Trust & Anti-AI
- **Layered trust architecture** (study of 507 contractor sites, Jan 2025–Mar 2026): licensing/credentials **at the top**, **LIVE Google reviews (not screenshots)**, **real photography (not stock)**, specific warranty/guarantee language. ✅ verified (3-0) · primary source but single founder-authored white paper.
  - Source: adgenius.com/research/contractor-website-analysis
- **Documented "looks template/AI" failure modes:** pasting static 5-star testimonials ("the *appearance* of social proof") and **stock photography**. Use a live Google review widget + real photos of the actual family/crew/trucks.
- **California specifics:** display CSLB license #, bonded & insured, and the **34-year heritage** prominently (heritage = built-in trust + anti-AI signal).
- **Honest gap (open question):** concrete *visual* anti-AI specifics (spacing systems, single-accent discipline, gradient/centered/emoji/fake-stat tells) were under-covered by surviving sources — needs dedicated design-source research.

## Caveats
- Source quality varies: form-length + typography + dedicated-service-page findings are best-sourced; contractor conventions and mobile-hero are vendor/agency blogs (corroborated); the 507-site trust study is rigorous-ish but a single self-published white paper.
- Time-sensitive: "Inter/Poppins fatigue" is 2026-dated; Aagaard/Expedia form cases are ~2010-2011 (mechanism-stable but dated).
