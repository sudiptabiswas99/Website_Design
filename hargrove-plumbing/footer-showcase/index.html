One footer, taken apart

The footer, as a showcase.

The Hargrove & Sons footer runs four effects at once: a sticky panel that pops up from behind the page and tilts into place, a slow meteor shower drifting behind the text, columns that stagger in on scroll, and a brass-into-cream gradient wordmark. Here each one is pulled out and shown on its own. Scroll to the very bottom to see all of it fire together, live.

EFFECT 01

The sticky pop-up reveal

The footer is the last thing on the page, pinned behind the content on a -100vh sticky track. As you scroll into the final stretch it is uncovered and rotates up from its bottom-left corner (12° to flat) so it feels like a panel snapping into place rather than just appearing. A requestAnimationFrame scrubber maps the tilt to how close you are to the very bottom, so it can never freeze half-done. Press replay to watch just the straighten.

EFFECT 02

Meteor-shower background

A vanilla port of the Aceternity “Meteors” effect: 22 tiny brass dots, each with a 60px trailing streak, launched on randomised delay and duration so the sky never loops in sync. They ride at a fixed 215° angle behind the footer text, glowing with a soft copper halo. The content sits on a higher z-index, so readability is untouched. Regenerate to reshuffle the field.

22 spans · ::before trail--delay / --dur custom propsrotate(215deg) driftz-index:0 behind content

EFFECT 03

Staggered column reveal

Each footer column starts 22px low and transparent and eases up into place, one after another, when the footer enters the viewport. An IntersectionObserver flips the switch and a per-column transition-delay creates the cascade. It is the same reveal used across the whole site, so the footer resolves in the same visual language as everything above it.

IntersectionObservertranslateY(22px) → 0--d staggered delaycubic-bezier(.2,.7,.2,1)

Gradient wordmark, brass rule & social lift
